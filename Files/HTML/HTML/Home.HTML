How OriGami was made

Origami is an ancient Japanese art form that involves folding paper into intricate shapes and designs. Believed to have originated in the 17th century, although the exact origins are unclear. Some historians believe that it may have been inspired by the Chinese art of paper folding. Though others suggest that it may have evolved from traditional Japanese paper-folding techniques. Regardless of its origins, origami has become a beloved art form around the world, with enthusiasts creating everything from simple animals to complex geometric shapes. Origami has been made by humans for centuries originating in Japan, where it was used for ceremonial uses and not for public satisfaction
